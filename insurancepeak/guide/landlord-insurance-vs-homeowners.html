Landlord insurance vs homeowners insurance

Key coverage differences when you rent out a property.

Quick note: This page is informational, not personal insurance advice.

Quick answer

Key coverage differences when you rent out a property. Use the checklist below to compare quotes on an apples-to-apples basis.

What to compare (apples-to-apples)

  • Same limits and deductibles on every quote
  • Same start date and payment plan (monthly vs pay-in-full)
  • Confirm exclusions, waiting periods, and claim process
  • Ask what discounts were applied (and which ones you don’t qualify for)

Typical cost drivers

  • Location/state rules and claims frequency
  • Coverage limits + deductible choices
  • Past claims / driving record / property risk (depending on type)
  • Credit-based insurance score where allowed (varies by state)

Simple ways to lower the cost

  • Raise deductibles only if you have a small emergency buffer
  • Bundle only if the total cost is truly lower (verify both policies)
  • Remove unnecessary add-ons; keep the protections that matter
  • Review at renewal — rates drift over time

FAQ

How many quotes should I compare?

At least 3. More is better when you have a special situation (recent claim, mobile home, landlord risk, etc.).

What’s the #1 mistake people make?

Comparing prices with different limits/deductibles. Make it apples-to-apples first.
